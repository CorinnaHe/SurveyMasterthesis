The AI system is trained on past credit decisions and looks for patterns in financial information, achieving an overall accuracy of approximately 71% in evaluation.

For each applicant, it considers the same factors that will be shown to you, such as annual income, outstanding debt, and interest rates.

The figures below show which applicant characteristics tend to influence the AI‚Äôs predictions most strongly. Features at the top of the plot are generally more important for the AI‚Äôs decision than those further down.

Each dot represents an applicant from the data used to analyze the model. Dots further to the left or right indicate a stronger influence on the prediction.

Dots on the right side indicate that the feature increased the model‚Äôs confidence in the respective class, while dots on the left side indicate that the feature decreased the confidence in that class. For example, in the plot for the poor class, points on the right support a prediction of poor creditworthiness, whereas points on the left speak against it.

The color of the dots indicates whether the feature value is relatively low or high (for example, lower vs. higher annual income).
